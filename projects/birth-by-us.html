Birth By Us — Empowering Maternal Care for Women of Color

Designing a doula-matching platform to address the Black maternal mortality crisis — from community research to live prototype.

Client
Birth By Us
Year
2024
Audience
Black & Brown birthing people
My Role
Researcher, designer & liaison
The Problem

Black mothers die at four times the rate of white mothers — regardless of income or education.

The disparity is driven by structural racism in healthcare. One of the most evidence-backed interventions? Doulas. But access to culturally competent doulas remains deeply unequal.

How I Think

Research first. Six focus groups before a single screen was designed.

1

Ground the problem in evidence, not assumptions

We started with literature — maternal mortality data, clinical evidence for doula care, and the socio-ecological model. We needed to understand structural forces before designing against them.

2

Go to the community — both sides of the equation

We ran 6 focus groups with two distinct audiences: birthing people and practicing doulas. A platform that only optimizes for the patient fails doulas. We needed to understand both simultaneously.

3

Let the data build the personas

The personas weren't invented — they were distilled. Aiysha came from a real pattern: a young low-income mother, away from family, who experienced miscarriage and couldn't find a doula. Karma came from a different pattern: an experienced doula frustrated by HIPAA burdens.

4

Design for the match, not just the search

Mothers didn't just need to find a doula — they needed the right one. The solution became a matching system surfacing doulas with shared lived experience.

Key Findings

Two audiences. Five needs each. One platform to bridge them.

Mothers want

  • Centralizing a place for information
  • Navigating costs and insurance
  • Cultural sensitivity and shared experience
  • Location-based doula discovery
  • Consistent support throughout

Doulas want

  • A professional doula network
  • A platform to connect with clients
  • Fair compensation structures
  • HIPAA-compliant intake paperwork
  • Clearer certification pathways
