About

Grounded in evidence, shaped by the world.

I studied Public Health at the University of California, Berkeley, with concentrations in epidemiology, data science, and design. My training in human-centered design through Berkeley's Fung Institute for Engineering Leadership fundamentally shaped how I approach problem-solving and continues to guide my consulting work today.

Throughout my career, I have worked at organizations across the healthcare ecosystem — including nonprofits, philanthropic foundations, biotech, and medtech companies. Each engagement has strengthened my technical expertise while broadening my perspective, allowing me to bring both analytical rigor and cross-sector insight to every role.

My perspective has also been shaped by living in five countries, speaking multiple languages, and living amongst diverse cultures. These experiences have taught me to approach complex challenges with curiosity, empathy, and adaptability. Above all, they remind me that behind every product, service, or system is a person whose experience matters. That human-centered mindset is what I strive to bring to every organization I work with.
